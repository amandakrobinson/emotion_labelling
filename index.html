What is this research about?

This experiment is part of a project examining the neural processes underlying visual object recognition and their relationship to behaviour.

You will be asked to undertake a simple behavioural task that will measure your perception and cognition. This may involve you watching simple visual stimuli on a computer display (e.g., coloured objects) and listening to sounds over speakers or headphones (e.g., low- or high-pitched tones). While you do this experiment, you will be seated at a desk and your keyboard presses and/or mouse movements will be recorded. You will need to do this experiment at a computer or laptop, not a mobile phone or tablet. You may also be asked to complete questionnaires relating to visual perception and handedness.

Your consent

Your participation is entirely voluntary. Once the experiment begins, you can choose to withdraw from the experiment at any time, without explanation or penalty. If you decide to withdraw from the experiment, please exit from the experiment (e.g., by closing the browser window), and the experiment will be terminated. If you withdraw, your information will not be used.

We will not disclose any identifying information about you, or provided by you during the research, without your written permission or as required by law. Fully anonymised data may be made available on public databases for use by other researchers.

What are the possible risks of taking part?

This experiment will involve looking at a computer monitor for up to an hour. There is a slight risk of fatigue, you will be given breaks in between trials. The risks are barely above those of everyday life, like watching a movie or playing a video game.

What are the possible benefits of taking part?

You will not receive direct health or other benefits from participation in this study. The data you provide, however, will help us to advance scientific understanding of how the brain regulates perception and cognition. You will receive payment for each hour of participation. If you would like to be informed of the results of this study, please contact project staff.

This study adheres to the Guidelines of the ethical review process of The University of Queensland and the National Statement on Ethical Conduct in Human Research. You are free to discuss your participation in this study with project staff (Dr Amanda Robinson, contactable on a.robinson4@uq.edu.au), and if you would like to speak to an officer of the University not involved in the study, you may contact the Ethics Coordinators on +617 3365 3924 / +617 3443 1656 or email humanethics@research.uq.edu.au.

Participant Task

The key guide will remain visible below each image. There is no feedback, but the next image will appear as soon as your response has been recorded.

Your responses are saved automatically. If you close the browser, you can return and resume where you left off.
